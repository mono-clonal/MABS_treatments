Cases 02

The second case is of a male individual diagnosed with multiple sclerosis. The diagnosis of Lhermitte's sign was made in July 2022, showing a positive result for IgG Oligo. The symptoms initially started with weakness in the left upper limb, and over time, there was the emergence of weakness in the lower limb and balance disturbances. MRI revealed the presence of plaques in the periventricular area (around the lateral ventricles and beneath the cortical surface).

Cases 03

The third case is a female patient who has been diagnosed with multiple sclerosis since 2019. The diagnosis was made through an MRI scan, which showed the presence of periventricular plaques. However, the analysis for cerebrospinal fluid abnormalities (Oligo bands) came back negative. The symptoms manifested as progressive limb weakness. After 4 years of the onset of the disease, the patient started receiving treatment with mAbs (monoclonal antibodies) in the form of Mabthera, with doses administered every 6 months.
