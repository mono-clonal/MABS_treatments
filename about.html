About

Autoimmune diseases are a type of disease in which the immune system malfunctions and begins to attack healthy tissues and cells in the body, leading to inflammation and damage in the targeted tissues. Common autoimmune diseases include lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, Hashimoto's thyroiditis, and others. These diseases are usually treated with immunosuppressive drugs, which target the autoimmune antibodies to reduce their activity. However, these drugs can also affect the immune system as a whole, making it more susceptible to infections. As a result, monoclonal antibodies have been developed as targeted biologics for the treatment of autoimmune diseases. They work to suppress the activity of autoimmune antibodies without affecting the immune system as a whole, thereby improving the effectiveness of treatment and reducing the side effects associated with traditional therapies
